In my spare time I love to do Carolina Shag which is the North and South Carolina state dance. I have been doing shag since I was 7 and a half and I have been competing since 2012.


During March of 2018 I was asked to join the Junior Shag Dance Team as an alternate. The coaches saw the effort I was putting in, along with my determination, perseverance, and dedication through weekend practices and endurance drills. They then decided I deserved a spot on the team to compete with JSDT at the USA Grand National Dance Championships in Atlanta, Georgia in May of 2018.


Since then, I traveled and competed on the team at GNDC (2018, 2019, and 2023), the US Open in Burbank, California (2018, 2019, and 2023), the French Open (2019), and performed and fundraised at many events and shag clubs across the east coast. Being able to bond with my teammates while traveling and spreading the dance we love will forever be some of my greatest memories.


Last year, I became one of the two captains helping to improve the team as a whole and mentor the newer Juniors on the team. I formed many lifelong bonds with many of them. These members knew I would be there if they needed help with our routines, shag technique, someone to talk to, advice or a shoulder to lean on, and even if they needed an assortment of items to be competition ready such as hair ties or bandaids.


Since then, I have aged off of the team, but will forever remember the amazing experiences that came my way. Although our practices were intense, I believe this team has shaped me to be the dancer I am today. The coaches and mentors I was able to learn from were an invaluable learning experience, and an honor that I am extremely grateful for. I will never forget the bonds that I have made and I will always continue to support the juniors, as they are the ones keeping our dance alive.
